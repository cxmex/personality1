5. People Management / HR Modules

Performance Management (Crehana Perform)

  • OKRs & Goals: Set company, team, and individual objectives; track progress with visual dashboards; cascade goals top-down
  • 360 Feedback: Multi-rater feedback from managers, peers, direct reports, and self-assessment
  • Competency Frameworks: Define role-specific competencies; measure against them during reviews
  • Performance Reviews: Configurable review cycles; automated reminders; calibration tools
  • Individual Development Plans (PIDs): Link performance gaps to learning paths automatically

Engagement / Climate (Crehana Engage)

  • eNPS Surveys: Net Promoter Score for employees -- "Would you recommend this company?"
  • Climate Surveys: Comprehensive organizational climate measurement
  • Pulse Surveys: Short, frequent check-ins (weekly/biweekly)
  • Recognition: Peer-to-peer recognition programs; kudos/badges
  • Sentiment Analysis: AI-powered analysis of open-ended survey responses
  • Real-time Dashboards: Engagement trends by team, department, location

Core HR (Crehana Core)

  • Onboarding: Automated welcome workflows; document collection; task checklists
  • Document Management: Digital storage; e-signatures; version control
  • Org Charts: Visual organizational structure; searchable employee directory
  • Leave Management: PTO requests; approval workflows; balance tracking
  • Internal Comms: Announcements; company-wide messaging

Recruitment (Crehana Attract)

  • ATS: Full applicant tracking from job posting to offer
  • Pipeline View: Kanban-style candidate progression
  • Scorecards: Standardized evaluation rubrics for interviewers
  • Intelligent Matching: Algorithm-based candidate-role fit scoring

Payroll & Attendance

  • Payroll: Automated payroll processing with tax compliance (Mexico, Colombia, Peru focused)
  • Attendance: Digital clock-in, biometric integration, shift management, overtime tracking

People Analytics

  • Cross-module dashboards aggregating data from performance, engagement, learning, and core HR
  • Custom report builder
  • Team/department/company-level views
  • Exportable data
Breadth vs Depth: Crehana covers an impressive breadth of HR functions. However, each module competes with specialized best-of-breed tools (Lattice for performance, Culture Amp for engagement, BambooHR for core HR, Greenhouse for ATS). The value proposition is integration, not necessarily depth in any single area.

6. AI Features

Hana -- Crehana's AI Assistant

Crehana has introduced an AI layer branded as "Hana" (likely a play on "humana") that operates across the platform:

  • Intelligent Search: Natural language queries across the entire HR platform -- "Who has a review due this week?" or "Show me engagement trends for the sales team"
  • 24/7 Employee Assistance: AI chatbot for employees to ask HR questions (policies, leave balances, benefits)
  • Task Automation: Automate repetitive HR workflows triggered by conditions
  • Knowledge Management: AI-powered company knowledge base with instant retrieval
  • Personalized Learning Recommendations: Based on role, skill gaps, performance review outcomes, and career goals
  • Sentiment Analysis: AI analysis of open-ended survey responses to detect themes, moods, and risk signals
  • Insights Generation: Automated insights and recommendations for HR leaders from aggregated data

7. Pricing

Enterprise / B2B Pricing (Crehana for Business)

No public pricing. Crehana uses a "contact sales" / "request a demo" model for all enterprise products. This is typical for mid-market/enterprise HR SaaS.

What We Can Infer

  • Minimum company size: Contact form segments start at "1-29 employees" but the platform clearly targets 50+ employee companies. Client list (Banorte, Santander, Honda) suggests 500+ employee sweet spot.
  • Modular pricing: Likely sold as modules (Core + Learn + Perform, etc.) with per-employee-per-month pricing
  • Estimated pricing: Based on comparable LatAm HR SaaS and the enterprise client profile, likely $5-15 USD per employee per month for bundled modules
  • Annual contracts: Almost certainly requires annual commitment for enterprise
  • Implementation fees: They emphasize "guided implementation" and "continuous strategic reviews" -- likely includes setup/onboarding fees

9. Weaknesses

What Users Complain About

  1. Customer support is poor (3.6/5): Slow response times, billing disputes, difficulty getting help -- the most consistent negative theme across reviews.
  2. Outdated course content: Some courses have not been updated in years; quality is inconsistent across the catalog.
  3. Feature depth vs breadth: They try to do everything (ATS, payroll, LMS, performance, engagement) but individual modules may lack the depth of specialized tools.
  4. No public pricing: "Contact sales" model creates friction and signals enterprise pricing that excludes SMBs.
  5. Billing/cancellation issues: Multiple reviewers report difficulty canceling subscriptions or unexpected charges.
  6. Premium paywall: Individual users report needing premium upgrades to access many courses.
  7. No funding since 2021: Suggests potential financial pressure; may be cutting corners on R&D or support.
  8. Identity crisis: Pivoting from B2C edtech to B2B HR platform creates confusion -- is it a learning platform or an HR suite?

Critical Gaps for Small Businesses

  • No SMB/micro-business tier: A 5-person retail store cannot use Crehana's business tools. Period.
  • No behavioral assessments: No DISC, Big Five, MBTI, or any personality/behavioral testing.
  • No AI coaching: Hana is an operational chatbot, not a coach. No personalized management advice.
  • No daily/weekly team pulse via simple endpoints: Their surveys are traditional forms, not quick daily questions via web links.
  • No conflict detection: No writing style analysis, behavioral pattern recognition, or proactive conflict flagging.
  • No game-based assessments: Traditional questionnaire-only approach.
  • Overkill for micro-businesses: Payroll, ATS, org charts are irrelevant for a 5-person team. Too much complexity.

Competitive Relationship

Verdict: Crehana is NOT a direct competitor. They serve a fundamentally different market segment (mid-market/enterprise vs micro-businesses). A 5-person retail store owner would never buy Crehana. However, Crehana is relevant as:
  • Aspirational reference: Their product breadth shows where the HR tech market is heading in LatAm
  • Potential future partner: If we scale, our assessment/coaching tools could integrate with their platform
  • Validation: Their $92M in funding proves investor appetite for LatAm HR tech

What We Can Learn from Crehana

  1. Spanish-first is non-negotiable: Crehana's biggest advantage is cultural/language fit. We already do this well.
  2. Integration story matters: Even Crehana struggles here ("coming soon"). If we nail WhatsApp/Slack/simple web integrations early, it's a differentiator.
  3. Learning + HR is powerful: Connecting assessments to development recommendations (e.g., "Your team member scores low on conflict resolution -> here's a micro-lesson") is something Crehana does and we should consider.
  4. Modular approach works: Companies buy one module, then expand. Our assessment tool could be the entry point, with coaching/pulse/analytics as expansion.
  5. Enterprise logos = credibility: Even if we target SMBs, having recognizable local business testimonials early is critical.
  6. Pivot cautionary tale: Crehana's B2C-to-B2B pivot created identity confusion. We should be clear about our market from day one.

Gaps We Fill That Crehana Does Not

  1. Micro-business accessibility: No setup, no demos, no minimum seats. A 5-person team can start in minutes.
  2. Behavioral assessments: DISC, Big Five, MBTI, Allport, Terman, Competencias -- game-based and questionnaire-based. Crehana offers zero.
  3. AI coaching for managers: "Your team dynamic shows X pattern, here's what to do" -- Crehana's Hana does not do this.
  4. Conflict prediction: Writing style analysis (Pennebaker/LIWC), behavioral trend detection, flight risk signals from daily pulse data.
  5. Game-based assessment: Our DISC game v2 (choice-based, no right answers) is something no LatAm platform offers.
  6. Daily micro-feedback: Quick web-link-based questions vs. formal survey tools. Better response rates, real-time data.
  7. Radically simple UX: Static HTML endpoints with zero login required vs. enterprise SaaS with onboarding.

Strategic Positioning

Our Positioning vs Crehana:

"Crehana is SAP for LatAm HR. We are the AI-powered people coach that fits in your pocket."

Crehana tries to be everything for big companies. We do one thing brilliantly for small teams: help managers understand their people and make better decisions -- using behavioral science, AI, and radical simplicity. A store owner does not need an ATS, payroll system, or LMS. They need to know who to hire, who is about to quit, and how to handle the conflict between their two best employees.
